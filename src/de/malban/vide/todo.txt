The sound emulation does not work correctly for the game VectorPilot (by Kristof Tuts). This is especially obvious for the player's shoot, that sounds quite different on the emulator and on a real vectrex.

Unlike most of the vectrex games, VectorPilot does not update the PSG register only once per frame (about every 20ms). Instead, it does modify the sound registers about every 5ms (roughly 4 times per frame) to output a high-quality sound.

By design, ParaJVE is only updating the sound wave at the end of every frame ; thus the "intermediate" writes to the PSG registers done by VectorPilot are ignored, plain and simple. To achieve a perfect rendering, the sound emulation sub-system must be refactored to update the waveform data whenever a PSG register is accessed (filling a temporary buffer, that will be consumed at the end of the frame, when the sound waveform is to be output).
